# CleanDesk Product Vision

## Mission
CleanDesk AI helps residential cleaning teams run the day from one calm mobile workspace: route, house details, cleaning workflow, photos, notes, follow-up, and owner visibility.

## Who It Is For
- Rachel, the owner, who needs to know where the team is and what needs attention.
- Emily, Becca, and Rachelle, the cleaners, who need a simple one-job-at-a-time flow while they are busy in homes.
- Noah or future office help, who may handle leads, scheduling, invoices, and follow-up.

## Problems It Solves
- Cleaners rely on Apple Calendar, Android screenshots, paper planners, and memory.
- Teams need shared schedules that work across iPhone and Android.
- Cleaners need job details before leaving: address, supplies, pets, alarms, access codes, special surfaces, and preferred products.
- Walkthrough notes, photos, and voice notes are scattered across paper and phones.
- Busy cleaners can click the wrong day or miss a warning.
- Owners need to know who is cleaning, driving, delayed, or in a walkthrough.
- Leads from Angi, Facebook, referrals, and the website need quick replies while the owner is cleaning.

## Product Principle
Do not make cleaners manage software while cleaning. The app should bring forward only what matters for the current job.

## Current Screens
- Today's Route: the dashboard becomes a route view centered on the next upcoming job.
- Schedule: weekly shared schedule grouped by cleaner.
- Clients: client binder with house details, products, access, pets, notes, photos, and visit history.
- Leads: draft-only AI replies for incoming leads.
- Walkthroughs: mobile walkthrough form for quote and house-profile capture.
- Cleaning Mode: focused active-job workflow.

## Core Workflow
1. Cleaner opens Today's Route.
2. Cleaner sees the next job first.
3. Cleaner checks supplies and alerts before leaving.
4. Cleaner taps Start Job.
5. Cleaning Mode shows timer, before photos, voice notes, supplies checklist, task checklist, and Complete Job.
6. Completion workflow captures after photos, notes, next clean, and future automation tasks.

## Phase Roadmap
### Phase 1: Core Prototype
Dashboard, Schedule, Clients, Leads, and Walkthroughs.

### Phase 2: Cleaning Mode
One job at a time. Timer, photos, notes, supplies, checklist, and completion flow.

### Phase 3: House Profile
Every house becomes a living record: pictures, pets, garage code, alarm, favorite products, voice notes, average cleaning time, average tip, history, problems, and maintenance.

### Phase 4: Owner Dashboard
Rachel can see cleaner status at a glance: Cleaning, Driving, Walkthrough, started time, and expected finish.

### Phase 5: AI
Add AI only after the team already loves the core workflow. AI should support replies, summaries, invoices, review requests, and posts without taking control away from the owner.

## Future Ideas
- Calendar sync for Apple and Google.
- Reminder texts.
- Smart quote suggestions from walkthrough notes.
- Photo-to-house-profile organization.
- Recurring task memory by house.
- Voice note transcription.
- Client review request drafts.
- Facebook post drafts from approved job photos.
